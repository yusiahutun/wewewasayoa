    07 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Mighty Potter has died following a fatal fall while competing at Fairyhouse in the WilllowWarm Gold Cup on Easter Sunday.

    Gordon Elliott's 6-5 favourite and four-time Grade One winner Mighty Potter suffered a heavy fall on the back straight, which has proved to be fatal. And the racing world has offered tributes to the 'classy' horse that grabbed headlines at Cheltenham.

    Mighty Potter had been one of the favourites heading into one of the biggest events on the calendar - but Mighty Potter failed to claim the win. One fan tweeted: "Mighty Potter has suffered a fatal fall at Fairyhouse race course today. Condolences to all connections. What a horse, devastating news for the racing community. Sleep well boy."

    READ MORE: Fears for 2023 Grand National with 'worryingly low' number of horses left

    Another added: "Mighty Potter is such a sad loss to the National Hunt scene and all those connected to him. I genuinely thought he could have the world at his feet heading into open company and was such a raw talent!"

    "It’s part of the game that doesn’t get easier and this one really hurt. You have those horses that you love in this game and he was one. Thoughts are with connections at this time," a third added. While a fourth said: "A classy horse, rest easy."

    And another added: "Our thoughts and sympathy are with all those who loved and cared for Mighty Potter and The Gunner Yeats. A desperately sad day."

    Flame Bearer was the eventual winner on Sunday, and Willie Mullins said: "He's a decent horse but he probably wouldn't have been my first choice.

    "I thought what he did in Thurles the last day was very good. He bucked out, jumped well, went a gallop and kept at it. If you have those novices jumping well in front, it's a huge help.

    "Sean went at it, went a good gallop and he jumped great. He got into a lovely rhythm and he did it well."
