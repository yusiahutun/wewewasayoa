The 10 All-Time Greatest Shooting Guards in NBA History

 Posted on September 13, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1575 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

In the game of basketball, the primary responsibility of a player who plays in the position of a shooting guard is to score the basketball on offense and steal it on defense. These players are usually strong and athletic and have the ability to get into the paint and get to the basket. Some of them have the ability to play in a variety of positions; such as a combo guard – a shooting guard who can act as a playmaker and a swinger – a goalkeeper who can act as a small forward. Here is a list of ten of the best shooting guards in NBA history who have become known for their ability to kick into high gear and have devastating effects on their opponents.

Greatest shooting guards of all time

10. Earl Monroe

Teams: Baltimore Bullets (1967-1971) and New York Knicks (1971-1980)
Titles: NBA Champion (1973)

Earl Monroe, nicknamed “Black Jesus” and “Earlla pearl” was a silky smooth shooting guard who is fondly remembered for his style. A four-time NBA All-Star was one of the most creative scorers of his time , as well as one of the greatest improvisers in the game, capable of handling the offense and causing damage without even having the ball.

The Philadelphia, Pennsylvania native played varsity basketball at Winston-Salem State University before entering the NBA in the 1967 NBA Draft as the second overall pick by the Baltimore Bullets . He went on to be named NBA Rookie of the Year in 1968 and was selected as the NBA’s first team in 1969.

9. Reggie Miller

Teams: Indiana Pacers (1987-2005)
Titles: None

Long before the likes of Steph Curry, KlayThompson and Ray Allen came along, a certain Reggie Miller was known and revered for his accurate three-point shooting abilities. Although he was unable to win a championship in his career, he will go down in history as one of the best shooting guards of all time thanks to his exploits on the field, most of which were against the New York Knicks, earning him the nickname “Knick Killer”.

Miller, who is widely considered the Pacers’ greatest player of all time, joined the prestigious 50-40-90 club in 1994 and is a five-time NBA All-Star.

8. Ray Allen

Teams: Milwaukee Bucks (1996-2003), Seattle SuperSonics (2003-2007), Boston Celtics (2007-2012) and Miami Heat (2012-2014)
Titles: 2× NBA champion (2008, 2013)

No player in NBA history created more fear of the ball than Ray Allen did. He essentially revolutionized the game by proving that a player could still deliver first-class value without having to dominate the ball. To do so, he was one of the purest shooters in the world, a trait that helped him set NBA records in both goals and three career goals.

Nicknamed Jesus Shuttlesworth after the role he played in the movie He Got the Game (1998), the California native played college basketball at the University of Connecticut before entering the league in the draw of the 1996 NBA as the fifth overall pick by the Minnesota Timberwolves who immediately traded him to the Milwaukee Bucks.

7. George Gervin

Teams: Virginia Squires (1972-1974), San Antonio Spurs (1974-1985) and Chicago Bulls (1985-1986)
Titles: None

In his day, George Gervin was as deadly as any other player when it came to scoring in basketball. He could score the ball at will, either from outside, by post, or by driving in line. His skill earned him the nickname “The Iceman” or, as color commentators would say, “Gervin, as cool as the other side of the pillow.”

The Phoenix Suns’ 40th overall pick in the 1974 NBA Draft led the league four times in scoring, made nine All-Star appearances and earned five All-Star First Team selections. the NBA.

6. Allen Iverson

Teams: Philadelphia 76ers (1996-2006, 2009-2010), Denver Nuggets (2006-2008), Detroit Pistons (2008-2009), Memphis Grizzlies (2009)
Titles: None

Nicknamed ‘The Answer’, Allen Iverson is not only going to be on the list of the greatest shooting guards of all time, but also on the list of the most influential players in NBA history. Despite being widely known for his signature Reeboks shoes, cornrow hairstyle, long shorts and upbeat style, Iverson was one of the toughest players to come up against. time.

The Hampton, a Virginia native who famously spent four months of a 15-year prison sentence behind bars before Virginia Governor Douglas Wilder issued a clemency order, played college basketball at the Georgetown University. He spent two years there before being selected as the first pick by the Philadelphia 76ers in the 1996 NBA Draft. Iverson went on to win the 1997 NBA Rookie of the Year award, led the league to score four times and became the shortest and lightest player to win the NBA Most Valuable Player award, among other accomplishments.

5. Clyde Drexler

Teams: Portland Trail Blazers (1983-1995), Houston Rockets (1995-1998)
Titles: NBA Champion (1995)

Clyde Drexler was a pure athlete famous for his serene and quiet demeanor off the court, fierce speed and peerless skill. The high-flying shooting guard was as sporty and quick as anyone who has ever played in the NBA. Many people have argued that if he hadn’t played in the same era as Michael Jordan, he would have been lucky to have had a bigger impact on the game.

Drexler played college basketball at the University of Houston where he, along with other future NBA stars such as Michael Young, Larry Micheaux and Hakeem Olajuwon, formed the famous “Phi Slama Jama” basketball fraternity. He then joined the NBA through the 1983 NBA Draft, becoming the Portland Trail Blazers’ 14th overall pick.

4. Dwyane Wade

Teams: Miami Heat (2003-2016, 2018 to present), Chicago Bulls (2016-2017), Cleveland Cavaliers (2017-2018)
Titles: 3× NBA champion (2006, 2012, 2013)

The City of Miami and Fans of the Basketball TeamDwayne Wade is the undisputed franchise leader in points, games, assists and steals, shots and shots. The Chicago native, who is primarily a shooting guard but can also play as a point guard when needed, has established himself in the league as one of the fastest and most difficult players to play. keep. He is also one of the best shot-blocking goalies of all time.

Wade, who put in one of the best individual NBA Finals performances of all time to help his team win a championship in 2006 was selected for 5th place in the 2003 NBA standings by the Heat. Before turning pro, he played college basketball for two years at Marquette University in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He was selected to the NBA’s First All-Star Team, having earned thirteen NBA All-Star appearances and the NBA Top Scorer award in 2009.

3. Jerry West

Teams: Los Angeles Lakers (1960-1974)
Titles: NBA Champion (1972)

Jerry West undoubtedly deserves a placelist of the greatest shooting guards of all time. He is described as a complete combo guard and rightly so, as he was very strong both in defense and in attack. Nicknamed Mr. Clutch, West was also renowned for his ability to come alive and score points late in the game when the pressure was on. There’s a reason the NBA logo is a silhouette of the legendary Laker.

West was born in Chelyan, West Virginia. He played college basketball at West Virginia University, where he won the NCAA Most Outstanding Player award in 1959, before being drafted 2nd overall by the Minneapolis Lakers, who were then moving to the city. of the Angels in the 1960 NBA draft. He led his team to nine NBA Finals, winning just one. For his performances in one of these Finals, he was named the Finals MVP despite being on the losing team, making him the only player in NBA history to achieve this feat.

2. Kobe Bryant

Teams: Los Angeles Lakers (1996-2016)
Titles: 5× NBA champion (2000-2002, 2009, 2010)

It’s no surprise that Kobe Bryant is considered one of the greatest shooting guards of all time. For many people who got to watch Michael Jordan play the game, Kobe Bryant was considered the best thing the NBA had to offer. A native of Pennsylvania, his game is inspired by the legend of the Chicago Bulls, and it’s safe to say he almost got mentioned in the same breath as Jordan. Bryant was famous for his ability to hit hard throws, which earned him a lot of criticism for his selfishness, but he spoke about his mental abilities. He then became the first guard to play 20 seasons in the NBA, becoming the third all-time leading scorer, surpassing Michael Jordan.

Bryant did not play college basketball as he stated for the NBA draft right out of high school. He was later picked 13th overall by the Lakers and became the all-time leading scorer. Among his other accolades are 18 NBA All-Star appearances, two NBA Goalscoring titles, and eleven NBA First Team selections, among others.

1.Michael Jordan

Teams: Chicago Bulls (1984-1993, 1995-1998), Washington Wizards (2001-2003)
Titles: 6× NBA champion (1991–1993, 1996–1998)

As written on the official NBA website, MichaelJordan is, by acclamation, the greatest basketball player of all time. With such a title, it’s no surprise that he tops the list of the greatest shooters of all time. He was able to achieve everything he did because he had the perfect combination of wit, athleticism, cunning and competitiveness. He could also play in a variety of positions, most often as a small forward or playmaker.

Jordan, who was nicknamed Air Jordan and his Airness for his performances in Slam Dunk competitions, played college basketball under coach Dean Smith at the University of North Carolina. He won the NCAA title in 1982, two years before entering the NBA as the Chicago Bulls’ third overall pick in the 1984 NBA Draft. year of the NBA in 1985, while winning many other awards during his career.
