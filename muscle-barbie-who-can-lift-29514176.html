    Muscle Barbie who can 'lift weight of lion' branded 'too hench' by cruel men

    A woman known as the 'Muscle Barbie' can deadlift the weight of a lion and she's only 5ft 4inches tall. Bodybuilder Julia Vins, 26, from Russia, is a force to be reckoned with as she's already broken three world titles.

    08 Oct 2024, 00:00

    A woman known as the 'Muscle Barbie' can deadlift the weight of a lion – and she's only 5ft 4inches tall.

    Bodybuilder Julia Vins, 26, from Russia, is a force to be reckoned with as she's already broken three world titles.

    She's also made a huge name for herself online, garnering one million followers on Instagram alone.

    But there's a downside to her fame – as the successful powerlifter has had to deal with bucket loads of trolls who poke fun at her muscly 10st physique.

    Thankfully, Julia doesn't care what people say and plans to keep building her "hench" figure.

    Since going to the gym at the age of 15, Julia continued to build her strength and is able to lift a whopping 180kg (28st) – the same weight as a lion or a pygmy hippo.

    But her amazing ability is looked down on by blokes, which she believes is due to "jealousy".

    “Some men can be intimidated by me and there are many people who wander around me at the gym when I lift such heavy weights," she said.

    “Many men say to me ‘with your face you could be a model, why have you disfigured your body?’

    “But often these comments are made by men who are not happy with their lives so I don't care about their opinion.”

    However, not all men are so cruel to Julia – who is known as the 'Muscle Barbie' – as boyfriend Sergei is supportive of her.

    He even works out alongside of the record breaking bodybuilder who wants to become as "muscly" as possible.

    "For me, there is no limit," Julia told The Sun.

    "I want to become as muscly as possible.

    "You can’t seem beautiful to everyone.

    "And everyone has a right to look and feel comfortable by expressing themselves in different ways."

    She previously shared she always works out with her makeup on, which is how the name 'Muscle Barbie' came about.

    "I always wear make-up, even when I'm squatting," Julia said.

    "I think it's important to look attractive in any situation.

    "I make sure my hair is done up in cute plaits and ribbons.

    "I like fashion too but it's very difficult to find nice clothes as my size is not standard."

    Back in 2014 the 'Muscle Barbie' entered the World Powerlifting Championships in Moscow in 2014 - and won the competition.

    Julia smashed three world record lifts that got her the gold place on the podium.

    She achieved her wins in the 180kg in the squat, 105kg in bench press and 165kg in the deadlift.

    The road to becoming a world record breaker was not an easy one for Julia, though.

    Her interest in powerlifting started when she was in school and found that she couldn't concentrate on the likes of exams.

    Despite having a "full schedule" at such a young age, Julia felt like she lacked a "true purpose" in her life.

    She spent a year trying to work out – but struggled with making progress.

    That was until she met a coach who helped her train and understand the "science" behind bodybuilding and powerlifting.

    "My body didn't feel like it was in sync with my soul - it just didn't feel right. It was then that I discovered powerlifting," the champion shared.

    "I believe that before you can achieve results, you must first have a solid understanding of the process.

    "Hard work and dedication are certainly important, but it's equally crucial to know how to use them effectively.

    “I made a lifestyle change by adopting a twice-daily meal routine, and it turned out to be the best decision I've made for my health.

    "Intermittent fasting has been a staple in my routine for many years, not only freeing up a considerable amount of time in my day, but also improving my overall well-being.

    Julia's motivation – and gorgeous 'Barbie' face – landed her modelling jobs as well as her progress in the gym.

    She trains every day of the week and “never takes a break."

    "This is my way of life,” the 'Muscle Barbie' previously admitted.
