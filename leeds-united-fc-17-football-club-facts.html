    Leeds United FC was established in 1919.

    Founded in 1919, Leeds United FC has a rich history spanning over a century. This Yorkshire-based club has become a prominent figure in English football and has experienced both highs and lows throughout its existence.

    The team plays their home matches at Elland Road.

    Elland Road has been the beloved home of Leeds United FC since This iconic stadium has a seating capacity of over 37,000 and has witnessed numerous memorable moments in the club’s history.

    Leeds United FC has won the English top-flight title three times.

    Leeds United FC has a proud record of securing three English top-flight titles. The club clinched the coveted championship in the 1968-69, 1973-74, and 1991-92 seasons, establishing themselves as a force to be reckoned with in English football.

    Leeds United FC has a passionate and devoted fan base.

    The supporters of Leeds United FC, known as the “Leeds faithful,” are renowned for their unwavering loyalty and passion for the club. Their fervent chants and unwavering support create a vibrant and electrifying atmosphere at every match.

    The club has a strong youth academy.

    Leeds United FC takes great pride in its youth academy, which has produced several talented players who have gone on to represent both the club and their respective national teams. The academy has played a crucial role in nurturing young talent and ensuring a sustainable future for the club.

    Leeds United FC enjoyed success in European competitions.

    In addition to its domestic achievements, Leeds United FC also left its mark on the European stage. The club reached the final of the European Cup in the 1974-75 season, showcasing their abilities against some of the best teams on the continent.

    Marcelo Bielsa, famously known as “El Loco,” has become an iconic figure at Leeds United FC. The Argentine manager’s tactical brilliance and emphasis on attacking football have endeared him to both players and fans alike.

    Leeds United FC has a strong rivalry with Huddersfield Town.

    The rivalry between Leeds United FC and Huddersfield Town is deeply rooted in their geographical proximity. Termed as the “Yorkshire Derby,” matches between these two teams are fiercely contested, showcasing the competitive spirit of the region.
