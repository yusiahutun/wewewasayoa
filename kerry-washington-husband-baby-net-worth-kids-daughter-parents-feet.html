    Kerry Washington Bio

    Kerry Marisa Washington was born on 31 January 1977 in the Bronx, New York City, and is the daughter of Earl Washington and Valerie Moss. Her father Earl was a real estate agent, and her mother Valerie, who is related to Colin Powell – a former US Secretary of State – is also an educational consultant and college professor. Kerry has a mixed pedigree, but her nationality is American. She attended the Spence School in Manhattan. After high school, she completed a double degree in sociology and anthropology and also completed her Phi Beta Kappa studies at George Washington University, Washington, D.C. in 1998. (formerly Columbian College/University). She also studied at the Michael Howard Studios in New York.

    Career

    Kerry Washington began her career in 1994 when she starred in Magical Makeover. Since then she has starred in some films including Standard Deviants (1996), Our Song (2000), Save The Last Dance (2001), Bad Company (2002), Human Stain (2003), She Hates Me (2004). From 2005 she only played supporting roles in films such as Mr. & Mrs. Smith (2005), Little Man (2006), The Last King of Scotland (2006), I Think I Love My Wife (2007), and starred in Tyler Perry’s For Colored Girls (2010), Django Unchained (2012), before she signed up for the ABC television series Scandal, in which she was the lead actress in 2012.

    She portrayed Olivia Pope, who runs her own company known as Olivia Pope Associates (OPA), a crisis management firm that protects the public image of its clientele (which at one point included the President of the United States). The still-running TV series has received many positive reviews as it was ranked 20th on the list of the highest-paid TV actors in 2012 with a net worth of $8 million. In 2013 it was named TV Star of the Year by Editor’s Magazine and nominated for an Emmy at the 65th Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Lead Actress in Drama.
