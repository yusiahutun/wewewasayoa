    Four Indianapolis teens killed in car crash for not wearing seatbelts

    Four teenagers from the central Indiana town of Indianapolis died on Wednesday evening when their vehicle struck a minivan. The four teens in the vehicle were killed and five others, including a fifth passenger in the vehicle and a family of four, were taken to a hospital for treatment, police said.

    04 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Four teenagers from the central Indiana town of Indianapolis died on Wednesday evening when their vehicle struck a minivan.

    The four teens in the vehicle were killed and five others, including a fifth passenger in the vehicle and a family of four, were taken to a hospital for treatment, police said. 

    None of those killed was wearing a seatbelt, but the fifth survivor was.

    Scroll down for video  

    Tragic loss: Mathew Howard Chambers, 17, died in a crash on Wednesday along with three others. He was not wearing a seat belt 

    The crash happened on State Road 37 in Morgan County near Waverly just before 7 p.m. on Wednesday.

    Police announced early Thursday that 17-year-old Mathew Howard Chambers, 18-year-old Lucas Edwin Kenworthy, 17-year-old Noah James Tex and 17-year-old Riley Davis Hurst died in the crash. All four were from Brownsburg.

    Police said that the one survivor in the car, Ryan Rickenbacker, was the only one wearing a seat belt.

    'It is devastating,' State Police Sgt. Curt Durnil said, calling it 'absolutely horrific for the families.' 

    Fox reports that  troopers are trying to find the reason for the crash. Police say they did not see any indications of alcohol being a factor.

    No seatbelts: Pictured here is the scene of the horrific crash. Police said that the one survivor in the car, Ryan Rickenbacker, was the only one wearing a seat belt

    Sad day: Noah Tex, 17, was another one of the victim's in the crash that has shaken an Indiana town and students and faculty at Brownsburg High School and the Harris Academy learning community where the students went to school 

    Officials from the local school district said counselors would be available on Thursday morning at Brownsburg High School and the Harris Academy learning community, where the students went to school.

    'We send our heartfelt thoughts and prayers to the families of these students,' the school district said in a statement. 

    State Road 37 was closed in both directions as officials investigate the crash. Brownsburg is about 15 miles northwest of Indianapolis.

    A Go Fund Me page was set up to help pay for the funeral expenses of Noah Tex. The site has raised $555 of the $8,500 goal so far.

    The school provided the following statement:  'We are saddened to report that on Wednesday evening a fatal crash claimed the lives of four students who attended Brownsburg High School and Harris Academy. We send our heartfelt thoughts and prayers to the families of these students. Counselors will be available at Brownsburg High School to provide additional support for our students during this difficult time.'
