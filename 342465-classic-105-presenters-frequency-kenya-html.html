    Classic 105 prides itself as the home of good times and great hits. The radio station broadcasts from Nairobi and is home to some of the country's most celebrated radio personalities. The Classic 105 presenters keep their listeners glued to the station through talk shows, news reports, traffic updates, and a blend of some "feel-good music." The broadcast is not limited to the capital city alone, and fans from different areas of the country also get to listen to their favourite shows and presenters.

    Classic 105 frequency is 105.2. Alternatively, you can listen to classic 105 live stream on the web. Most audiences are usually curious about this incredible radio team that leaves them entertained, and some wish to have a peek of classic 105 presenters photos.

    From discussions on everyday topics to business and sports updates to professional advice on love and marriage, these radio kings and queens no doubt answer many listeners' questions. Here are all classic 105 FM Kenya presenters.

    Terry Muikamba

    The presenter is not new on radio airwaves. She features in the Evening Drive, a show that she hosts alongside her co-presenter, Mike Mondo, airing every Monday to Friday. She has also served as a presenter in several media houses, including alongside Willis Raburu at Hot 96.

    Referring to herself as "Miss Sunshine," Terry also doubles as a lawyer and voice over artist. She is an alumnus of Bishop Gatimu Ngandu Girls high school, and graduated from the University of Nairobi in 2015, with a Bachelor of Laws (LL.B). The media personality is the firstborn in their family.

    Terry Muikamba is not married and has no children. She, however, is a proud aunt.

    Maina Kageni

    Maina arguably tops the list of the most popular Classic 105 presenters and is also among the country's leading radio personalities. He, alongside the comical Mwalimu King' ang' i, has captured the hearts (and ears) of many listeners in Kenya and beyond. Their show titled "Maina and King' ang' i in the morning" is among the most listened to and plays in almost every private vehicle or matatu transporting people to their respective destinations or work.
