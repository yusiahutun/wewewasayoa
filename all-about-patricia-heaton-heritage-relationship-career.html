Where was Patricia Heaton born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Patricia Helen Heaton is an Irish-American producer and performer who was born on March 4, 1958 in Bay Village, Ohio. Her nickname is Patty. Her mother is Patricia Heaton and her father, Chuck Heaton, was a sportswriter for The Plain Dealer.

She was raised a devout Roman Catholic. Her three sisters are Sharon Heaton, Frances Heaton and Alice Heaton. Her brother, Michael Heaton, is also part of her family. He is Minister of Culture and columnist for Viernes Magazine.

Heaton graduated from St. Raphael Catholic Elementary School in Bay Village and attended Bay High School in 1976. During his high school years, he was a member of the Bay Rockets Marching Band and the Rockettes Drill Team.

Heaton was also a member of the Stage Band and sang in the choir. While attending Ohio State University, she became a sister to Delta Gamma. Heaton moved to New York in 1980 to study theater with William Esper.

In 1987, while working with New York theater master William Esper, she sang in the chorus of Don’t Get God Started. Off-Broadway, or Stage Three, was founded by her and other students. When one of Stage Three’s plays screened in Los Angeles, she caught the attention of a casting director.
