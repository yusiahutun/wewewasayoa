    We all know how important it is to take care of our teeth as we age, but doing so becomes even more important once dentures come into the mix. One of the most important parts of denture care is finding the best denture adhesive for you. Denture adhesives come in many forms and are designed to help keep them firmly in place as you eat, speak, smile, laugh, and go about your day. Aside from keeping your dentures secure, adhesives can also keep food from getting stuck in gaps between your teeth and your gums, preventing plaque build-up and bad breath. 

    Types of Denture Adhesives

    When it comes to choosing a proper adhesive, you have more options than you might think. While most people tend to associate denture adhesive with creams, other variations, such as denture powders, denture strips, and denture seals, are also available. Keep reading for more on each type to find out which is right for you. 

    Denture adhesive creams: Easily the most popular adhesive on the market today, denture creams, also known as denture glue or paste, can be applied directly to your artificial tooth frame and come in a convenient tube that resembles toothpaste. Though this type of adhesive is highly effective at keeping dentures in place, one common complaint is that creams can spill over the sides, making for a messy, unsightly experience.

    Denture adhesive powders: Another kind of denture adhesive comes in the form of a powder. Easier to clean than creams and pastes, denture powder is sprinkled directly onto wet dentures, which are then applied to the roof or floor of the mouth. While many are fans of the powder option for its clean application, it’s important to note that some powders don’t last as long as other forms of adhesive might, meaning you may have to reapply more than once a day. 

    Denture adhesive strips: For a mess-free option, denture strips can be a great pick. The newest type of adhesive to hit shelves, these strips are applied directly to the top of dentures which are then secured inside your mouth. These adhesives do require some customization, however — you’ll have to cut them down to size for your teeth by measuring them against your dentures. Though they require a little extra effort to set up, they just might wind up fitting you the best, making them well-worth it.

    Denture seals: Combining the cleanliness of a strip with the grasp of a cream are denture seals. These solutions, which are applied to the top of your denture, mold to the shape of gums, creating a suction-tight grip. These beloved adhesives are a favorite of those looking for the most secure fit. 

    How to Remove Denture Adhesive

    While you want to make sure you use a strong denture adhesive to keep them in place, it’s equally important to be sure to fully remove stuck-on adhesive from your dentures at the end of the day to prevent any bacterial growth or deterioration of the denture.  

    To do so, begin by removing your dentures from your mouth. The easiest way to do this is by swishing some warm water around and gently rocking your dentures from side to side to loosen them for removal. Next, use a soft bristle brush ($7.79, Amazon) and warm water to gently scrub any remaining adhesive from both your artificial teeth and your mouth — especially around the roof and your gums. From there, all that’s left to do is follow up with your regular denture hygiene by cleaning and soaking them n a cleansing solution ($5.88, Amazon) and storing them in a glass of water so they don’t dry out.
