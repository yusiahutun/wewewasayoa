    Web Notifications On Mac

    Mac users can easily access their notifications from the desktop if they don’t want to miss any important updates. This is the place where you can keep up with updates within a few clicks. However, many users will find this option disturbing.

    While this seems a great option to keep updated from your favorite sites, you will receive tons of different alerts. Some of them will be beneficial, but you will find others not relatable. In most cases, it’s challenging to proceed with important work when you are disturbed by the sound.

    Furthermore, there is an additional issue with this. Cybercriminals often use them to send spam that leads to suspicious websites. If you are worried about spam or receiving tons of unnecessary Notifications On Mac, there is an easy fix. There are a few ways to disable the notifications, so we will show you the easiest ones. There is an option to customize the settings for displaying notifications from your preferred browser. Or you can choose to go to Mac System Preferences and start from there. Whether you use Safari or another browser, we have you covered with the full instructions. This post provides you with essential tips, so let’s get started!

    Turn off Notifications on Chrome

    Google Chrome makes it complicated for users to turn off the notifications on the desktop. This can be explained by the fact that it engages users and makes them spend more time on the app. You can turn off Chrome notifications on a mac with our simple instructions. First, you need to launch the browser. Start Chrome and click on the three spots in the upper right corner.

    From the list, pick the Settings option. Locate the advanced settings at the bottom of the page. In Privacy Security, locate the Content Settings and select it. Go to Notifications On Mac. Click on the three spots to reach the settings and decide if you wish to enable or remove notifications.

    Control the Notifications from Safari

    Users can restrict the notifications straight from the settings menu of Safari. If you need to adjust or disable the Notifications On Mac, this is the easiest and most convenient method. Start by launching the Safari window. Click on Safari and proceed to Preferences. Find the option Websites under the Preferences, and click. Look for Notifications on the left sidebar and click it. There is an extensive list of sites that have the notification option permitted. Enter the drop-down menu and click Deny.

    Repeat this on all sites that you don’t want to get notifications from. If you wish to remove a particular website from the list, pick the Remove button. By following these steps, you can prevent the websites from sending alerts. If you don’t want to be asked regarding permissions anymore, untick the “Allow websites to ask for permission to send push notifications” field.

    Turn off notifications on Mozilla Firefox

    Start Firefox and go to the settings menu by clicking on the three dots in the upper right corner. Pick the Preferences option. On the menu on the left side, pick the Privacy Security option. Scroll down to find the Permissions. Spot the Notifications On Mac and select Settings. Control notification permissions and remove URLs from the list. If you don’t want to receive more requests for permissions, scroll below the list and find the option to block new requests.

    How to Manage Notifications on Mac

    Controlling your Notifications On Mac is very simple with this method. We know that all those unnecessary notifications will clutter your notification center, so here is the step-by-step guide to turning off Chrome notifications on mac.

    Click on the Apple logo at the top of the screen. Pick the System Preference from the menu. Click on Notifications and pick Safari. Here, you can choose whether you prefer None, Banners, or Alerts. Users can decide to hide the alerts on the bar and notification center. Also, there is a possibility to turn off the sound. If you are worried about hackers stealing your data, add additional protection with AdGuard. This app blocks ads and different types of malicious activity and works across all browsers on Mac.

    Additional tips for managing Notifications on Mac

    If you have an upcoming meeting, you don’t want to be disturbed by the annoying sound. But also, screen sharing means that the participants will be able to see the pop-up Notifications On Mac. If you’re going to turn off the alerts for a short period for privacy reasons, we recommend that you use the Do Not Disturb button. This option will mute the sound, and the notification bar turns gray. In cases when you get important alerts, there is an option to access them later from the notification bar. Besides, you can control the settings according to your specific needs. You can choose to hide notifications when mirroring to other devices or display a repeated call.

    Final words:

    We hope that this guide helped you turn off those annoying alerts. Your messages will stay private and you won’t be disturbed by the sound. The best thing is that you can customize the settings and adjust them to your specific needs. We covered a few alternative methods of hiding the Notifications On Mac that take a few clicks so that you can pick the most convenient one. As a Mac user, you can now enjoy your activities without being disturbed.
