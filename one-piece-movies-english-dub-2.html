One Piece Movies English Dub Release Status &amp; Other Latest Updates

 Posted on July 6, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  525 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

In the vast ocean of anime, One Piece stands tall with its tales of adventure and treasure. Now, imagine those adventures on the big screen – that’s right, the One Piece movies! Out of the 15 movies released so far, only a handful have been treated to the English dub treatment. Fans have been patiently waiting for the dub magic to touch the remaining films. So, what’s the latest scoop on these dubbed treasures, and is there hope for the ones left waiting in line? Let’s set sail together as we uncover the latest buzz and explore the future possibilities of One Piece’s English-dubbed movie collection.

One Piece Movies English Dub Release Status ( as of December 2023)

The current status of One Piece dubbed movies is a mix of progress and limitations. While a few movies and TV special episodes have successfully received dub treatment, there are constraints on older releases. Unfortunately, it’s improbable that the earlier and vintage movies will be dubbed, as their absence in the release timeline suggests no such plan. None of the early One Piece movies received an official English release in any form. Consequently, the only available option to watch them is through fansubs.

However, the silver lining lies in the recent releases, where new One Piece movies have the potential to receive dubbed versions. The likelihood of dubbing primarily hinges on the timing of the movie’s release. As of December 2023, the makers don’t seem to have any plans to begin the dubbing for any of the One Piece movies. As for TV Specials, a notable set including 3D2Y, Adventure in Nebulandia, Heart of Gold, Episode of Sabo, Episode of East Blue, and Episode of Skypeia has been successfully dubbed, showcasing a positive trend in extending dubbing efforts to specials with movie-like durations.

One Piece Dubbed Movies

Out of the 15 One Piece movies, only 6 have been dubbed.  In July 2023, the dubbed versions of “One Piece Film Red,” “Film Gold,” and “Stampede” were officially released on Crunchyroll.
