    What is the Tadpole Galaxy?
    The Tadpole Galaxy, also known as UGC 10214, is a barred spiral galaxy located approximately 420 million light-years away in the constellation Draco. It is named for its distinctive tadpole-like appearance, characterized by a bright head and a long, faint tail of stars.

    How was the Tadpole Galaxy formed?
    The Tadpole Galaxy is believed to have been formed through a gravitational interaction with another galaxy, resulting in the elongation of its stars and the creation of its unique shape. This process, known as galactic tidal forces, has contributed to the captivating appearance of the Tadpole Galaxy as observed today.
