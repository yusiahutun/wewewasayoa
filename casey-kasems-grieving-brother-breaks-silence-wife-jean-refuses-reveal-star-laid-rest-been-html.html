    The grieving brother of TV star Casey Kasem has branded his dead sibling's wife 'pure evil' and said her reluctance to reveal where Casey's body will be laid to rest is a 'sick joke.'

    Mouner Kasem hit out at Jean Kasem – Casey's wife of 34 years – after it emerged the family has not been told where the TV personality will be buried and amid reports his remains have been shipped to Canada.

    In his first interview on the death of his brother the 78-year-old told MailOnline: 'It's a sick joke from a sick woman. It's a sick joke that Jean would say she lived in Jerusalem, Israel on the death certificate and it's a sick joke that she said his body is to be shipped to Montreal in Canada, a place Casey has never even been to.'

    This comes as Casey's daughter Kerri says she suspects that her father may have already been buried in Los Angeles in a secret funeral service.

    Brother: Mouner Kasem, the brother of Casey Kasem, is pictured here in February with his niece Kerri

    Kerri revealed that her 'sources' told her that Jean Kasem and daughter Liberty were seen attending a funeral over the weekend.

    Casey, the radio host of 'American Top 40' and voice of animated television characters like Scooby-Doo's sidekick Shaggy, died June 15 at a hospital in Gig Harbor, Washington.

    He was 82 and suffering from dementia, and his death followed a lengthy battle over his care between Jean and his three adult children from his first marriage.

    Last week, a judge granted Kerri a temporary restraining order preventing Jean from cremating his remains but the whereabouts of his body has since become a mystery.

    A lawyer for Kerri said that when he went to give a Tacoma funeral home a copy of the restraining order, he was informed it no longer had the remains.

    It's believed the body was moved to a funeral home in Montreal, Canada, but the home there has refused to confirm this.

    And now the family is wondering whether Casey's body may have been moved to LA for a secret burial.

    Either way Mouner, who with his wife Mary jetted to Casey's bedside during the last days of his life joining the star's three children Kerri, Julie and Michael at St. Anthony's Hospital in Gig Harbor, Washington, said Jean's actions are piling more misery on his family while they deal with the shock of Casey's tragic death.

    Angry: Mouner Kasem hit out at Jean Kasem, pictured with Casey in 1997, after it emerged the family has not been told where the TV personality will be buried and amid reports his remains have been shipped to Canada

    'We would like to know where Casey is buried,' he said. 'We want to be able to visit his grave and put flowers on the gravestone, you know on his birthday or on the day he died.

    'But our fear is that that may never come. I truly believe that Jean will not tell us.

    'Taking into account her behavior during this last year it is really not surprising that she won't tell us where the body is buried and I don't know if she ever will.

    'I don't believe there is a law that says she has to tell us, but it would be the right, human, decent thing to do for the sake of our family.'

    Mouner said he only hopes Jean changes her mind and said the Kasem family would be 'grateful' if she did.

    But he added: 'I can tell you that that is not going to happen. If she can throw a pound of hamburger meat at Kerri then she's not going to tell us where the body is.

    'If she can take Casey out of the convalescent center where he was doing stellar and take him out at 2.30 in the morning, then that shows what kind if woman she is.'

    Mouner said he was always very close with his older brother and said the star often came out to see him and his wife Mary in Grand Blanc, Michigan.

    But Jean and her daughter Liberty, despite many invites, never made the trip with Casey.

    Mouner said: 'After four years of them being together we never saw Jean, she never came to the house, we never talked. She was never part of our family, she never participated in anything.

    Has he been buried? Casey's daughter Kerri says she suspects that Jean Kasem, pictured in May this year, and daughter Liberty were seen attending a funeral over the weekend

    'She did exactly what she wanted and got everything she wanted, she had a baby right away, that's what she wanted.

    'I've always been very close to Casey but Jean was never a part of that.'

    His wife Mary said: '... this is not a family feud, it's Jean's vengeance and vindictiveness and hatred for his three oldest kids, that's what this is all about, she hates them.

    'She talks about a tacit relationship but who poisoned it and who continues to poison it. She's the only one doing poisonous things.

    'We are at the point where nothing surprises us any more.

    'She's pulled all the rabbits out of her hat and I don't exactly know what other evil things there is left for her to do.'

    However, at a court hearing in early June, Jean claimed she and her husband had lived in 'sheer horror' of Casey's daughter Kerri and accused her of wanting her father dead.

    Jean Kasem stormed out of court after Judge Daniel Murphy revoked an earlier decision ordering doctors to continue artificially feeding the ailing star.

    'You have blood on your hands,' she told the judge, adding that her husband expressed his wish to live using eye and eyebrow movements during a five-minute meeting at the hospital in early June.

    'They had to pry his hands off me,' she told the judge under penalty of perjury.

    Speaking of Kerri, as she pounded her fist on a courtroom table, she said: 'We have lived in sheer horror of this girl.'

    '(Kerri) has had in her possession her father less than a week and decided to stop all medication and kill him,' testified Jean.

    Mouner says he has now erased Jean out of his contacts list and hopes never to speak with her or see her again.

    He added: 'Jean has no pride, no personal pride and clearly has no respect for the husband she has claimed to love nor for her family.

    'She is unbelievably evil, pure evil.'

    The family now hope that Jean has been playing an elaborate game and that Casey has actually been buried in LA.

    Restraining order: Last week a judge granted Casey's daughter Kerri, pictured, a temporary restraining order preventing Jean from cremating his remains but the whereabouts of his body has since become a mystery

    Mary added: 'What kind of funeral could she have had, how many people attended, two, three?

    'We had a memorial the weekend after Casey died for close friends and family and there was a huge memorial in Lebanon for him, so with regards to a funeral we have kind of really had our funeral.

    'We knew from the get go that she would not invite us or allow us to attend Casey's funeral and we came to grips with that, we believe that his spirit is more important than his body and that he will be with us for a very long time and we have each other to remind us of him.

    'The girls and Michael made a conscious decision that they were not going to fight over the body, this was a tacky thing to do.'

    Earlier today in a television interview with Access Hollywood Kerri Kasem and sister Julie spoke of their heartache over the whole row.

    Kerri revealed she had heard that Jean and daughter Liberty were seen on their way to a funeral over the weekend, likely in Los Angeles.

    'It could have been a different funeral but that's the closest thing we have gotten,' she said.

    'The last I heard from a source is that she (Jean) is probably playing games. This is not a shocker, she put on the death certificate that she was living in Jerusalem for 18 years, she also told the funeral home that she was going to take him to France and put him there, then no let's change that to Montreal and put him there. It's just games, she thinks she's gonna upset us and that's just not gonna happen. It's sad.'

    Abuse? The Santa Monica police department is currently investigating claims of elder abuse against Casey, pictured in 2003, in his final months

    Sister Julie said: 'The silver lining is that we got to be with our dad at the end of his life and we know that his spirit is here and that's the most important thing.'

    The Santa Monica police department is currently investigating claims of elder abuse against Casey in his final months.

    Kerri told Access Hollywood: 'The minute my dad died everything (control) went back to my step-mother (Jean) so I had nothing to do with where he was at, where he was going to be buried.

    'The Santa Monica PD wants the body for autopsy, not the Kasem family. We know what went on, we know of the medical records, we know what happened.'

    Kerri added that there's 'overwhelming evidence' of how her father died but it's up to the District Attorney's office whether to bring any kind of prosecution.

    Sister Julie added: 'We will never know where he is buried unless we can get a private detective or lawyers involved or a court order like we had to do when he was alive, it's a game.'

    The Kasem family are now campaigning for a bill to be submitted into law to allow families the right to ask where loved ones are buried.

    Kerri has joined forces with Assemblyman Mike Gatto (D-Los Angeles) in proposing new legislation.
    Gatto's bill would require conservators inform relatives if someone is hospitalized for acute care, or dies, and disclose funeral arrangements and burial location.

    Gatto says the bill also provides 'reasonable accommodations' for adult children to visit their parent.

    'This is about keeping families together,' Gatto told LA radio news KABC.

    'I think the most important thing, of course, is to make sure that children get to see their mom or dad if that person is getting toward the end of days.

    'It's the most important thing I've ever done in my life and I just hope this helps so many families that are going through this,' said Kerri.
