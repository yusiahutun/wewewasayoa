    How to Apply an e.l.f. Primer?

    There are 3 types of e.l.f. primers— creamy, gel-based, and misty. Applying them may differ, but the idea is to get full coverage and a protective barrier between your skin and makeup.

    Here are the steps you can follow:

    Step 1: Take a pearl-sized amount on your fingertips.

    Step 2: Warm it a little by rubbing it between your fingertips.

    Step 3: Start applying gently from next to your nose or under-eye areas where pores are visible.

    Step 4: Keep blending and spreading to other sides with your fingertips or blender.

    Step 5: Once the primer has dried or absorbed, you can apply makeup.

    How to Choose the Best e.l.f. Primer?

    • Skin Type:

    It’s half the battle won if you know your skin type well. Especially if you are dealing with skin irritation, visible pores, premature fine lines, or damaged skin, checking the ingredients before buying becomes all the more important. For example, tea tree oil is non-comedogenic and great for fighting acne and reducing scars. But, if you have acne-prone and sensitive skin, it may worsen the irritation or even cause rashes.  So be mindful before you choose a product.

    • Finishing Look:

    Although a matte finish is well-loved as it creates a smooth base for your makeup, there are primers with dewy and shimmery finishes too. A must-try for those with dull skin, primers with a dewy or shimmery finish add a radiating touch to your skin that makes your complexion glow without going over the top. e.l.f. GLOW PRIMER STICK and e.l.f. ILLUMINATING FACE PRIMER are great options if you want to add a subtle shine to your daily look.

    • Gripping Power:

    If your primer doesn’t grip well, your makeup will not last long. This is why choosing the right texture and formula is imperative to get that perfect gripping base on your skin. Also, knowing your skin type plays a crucial role in this. Ideally, creamy textures grip better on dry and normal skin, whereas gel-based and water-based are ideal for oily, combination, and acne-prone skin.

    • Added Features:

    e.l.f. primers don’t just prime but come with extra skin-loving benefits. If you have dry skin, choose an ultra-hydrating primer with ingredients such as squalene or hyaluronic acid. If you have acne-prone skin, e.l.f. has a primer that reduces breakouts too! So make the most of these benefits for your skin.
