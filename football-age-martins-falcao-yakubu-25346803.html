    Six footballers accused of being older than they are - from Radamel Falcao to Martins

    Joseph Minala has found a new club seven years after he was accused of being 42 as a teenager - but he's not alone. The star was playing for Lazio at the time and while an investigation was launched, no wrongdoing was ever found.

    04 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Joseph Minala has found a new club seven years after he was accused of being 42 as a teenager - but he's not alone.

    The star was playing for Lazio at the time and while an investigation was launched, no wrongdoing was ever found.

    But it's not the only case of questions being raised, and footballers have occasionally found themselves the subject of suspicion.

    Often these accusations have been nothing more than rumours, and soon find themselves spreading rapidly on social media.

    But such rumours have forced some stars to deny they are older than the fans believe.

    And Daily Star Sport is looking at six players who have been accused of being older than they really are...

    Obafemi Martins

    At 37-years-old Obafemi Martins is still playing professional football for Wuhan in the Chinese Super League.

    However, he is perhaps best known for his stint in the North East at Newcastle United. And at the time he was accused of being 28 when he was 21.

    In 2006, an administrative error at the Nigerian FA saw his birth date placed on 1 May 1978, making him 28.

    The error, which Nigeria admitted to, placed Martins' transfer to Newcastle in doubt, and the striker temporarily quit the national team as a result.

    Martins said: "It is clear that they do not know anything about my age.

    "When this story came out in the papers, I felt that I have to do something about the situation.

    "So many false things have been said about me in the past, including stories that I am a Cameroonian.

    "I will call the NFA chairman and discuss this matter with him because it is making me very unhappy."

    Radamel Falcao

    Rayo Vallecano's No.3 is a Madrid hero once more after his strike consigned Ronald Koeman to the sack at Barcelona over the weekend.

    However, after the Colombian left the Spanish capital for Manchester United in 2014, there were accusations over his age.

    Falcao's primary school dug up documents that purported he was born in 1984, not 1986, but these claims were soon dismissed when a Colombian newspaper published his birth certificate.

    The Colombian himself tweeted: "I have been surprised with stories in the media about my age, they are totally ridiculous. I want to deny these claims and end this issue once and for all."

    United said at the time: "We were aware of the rumours but it was never an issue for us."

    Diego Costa

    Diego Costa is back in Brazil for the twilight of his career after spells at numerous sides across Europe.

    However, wherever Costa pops up there is always a shock among fans to find out he is generally younger than they expect the former Spanish international to be.

    "17 year-old Diego Costa looks like he's in a teen drama being played by a 34 year old," one fan joked upon seeing a not-so-fresh-faced Costa in his youth.

    Amazingly, Costa is the same age as Sergio Aguero, Javier Hernandez, and Adam Lallana.

    Yakubu

    "Feed the Yak and he will score," chanted Everton fans during Yakubu's glory days in the late 2000s at Goodison Park.

    The goals next to Yakubu's name was the only number that Toffee's fans cared about, but there were questions over the age of the Nigerian striker.

    Harry Redknapp once joked that Yakubu was 49 after the veteran striker was forced into an extended spell on the sidelines following an injury.

    David Moyes came under fire when he told the Guardian: "He's only 25, albeit a Nigerian 25, and so if that is his age he's still got a good few years ahead of him."

    He later said: "What I said has been taken totally out of context. I have never before been accused of being a racist, that idea is totally abhorrent to me."

    At 35-years-old Charlie Adam is a year younger than Manchester United's returning hero Cristiano Ronaldo.

    But Adam has at times been teased for looking beyond his years, with some claiming the Dundee midfielder looks 'ten years older' than the Portuguese superstar.

    "Not sure what’s crazier, the fact Charlie Adam is only 35 or the fact that he’s younger than Ronaldo," one fan tweeted.

    Another added: "Still amazes me that Charlie Adam is a year YOUNGER than Cristiano Ronaldo."

    Perhaps the most famous example on this list, Joseph Minala was accused of being 42-year-old as a youngster in the Lazio academy.

    When Minala was 17, an African football website suggested that the Cameroonian midfielder was actually 25 years older than he claimed to be.

    The accusation led to an official investigation, but nothing was ever found to suggest Minala had been lying.

    Minala never broke into the Lazio side, but he is now back in Italy, and he turns out for Serie D side Lucchese 1905.
