Her Breakthrough Role Almost Did Not Happen

In January 2018, the actress responded to an open casting call on Twitter for Steven’s upcoming film adaptation of the musical West Side Story. Initially, her instinct was to step back because the local production was already one hell of an experience for her.

Zegler almost did not take the role but eventually responded by tweeting videos of herself singing I Feel Pretty and Tonight. Ultimately, she landed the role among over 30,000 applicants in January 2019.

The period of the auditions was one year, and she had countless sleepless nights. She was moving from junior to senior high school and trying to figure out college.

Rachel, who did not have an official representative then, called Kristie Mascosko Krieger, a producer of the film, to inform her that she was moving forward with college applications.

The producer thought it would be wise to do so, and Zegler interpreted that she was not getting the part. So, she began to audition for Broadway shows and other films. She devised a plan to study musical theater at Montclair State University.

But fate had other things planned for Rachel, and eventually, Spielberg noticed her. So when she sang for him in person, he melted, and she landed the role.

In December 2021, the film was released and was hit at the box office and received good reviews. She also made $38.5 million at the box office.
