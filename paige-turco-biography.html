BIOGRAPHY
Raised in Massachusetts, Paige Turco dreams of becoming a prima ballerina. An injury forced him to give up the sport at the age of 14 to study acting. Freshly graduated from the University of Connecticut, she began her career on the daily soap opera Hate and Passion , in which she played Dinah Morgan Marler, a teenager troubled by her adoption. The following year, she moved to the ABC soap opera The Force of Destiny . However, above all, she wants to try her luck on the big screen. This is how she found herself in the casting of Ninja Turtles 2 and 3 , in the role of April O’Neil, the best friend of the 4 superheroes.

In the 2000s, the young woman became one of the protagonists of American Gothic , Gail Emory, determined to solve the murder of her parents. Due to lack of audience, this controversial fantasy series ceased to be broadcast at the end of the first season. Her performance being noticed, however, she then appeared in the Fox program, La vie à Cinq , then in several feature films: Urbania , Dead Dogs , Rhinoceros Eyes and Maxi Papa , the Disney comedy with Dwayne Johnson .

In 2007, Paige Turco guest-starred in the second season of the legal drama Damages , as the wife of David Purcell, played by William Hurt . Finally, after a brief appearance in the first season of Person Of Interest , she returned the following year in the shoes of negotiator Zoe Morgan, a recurring character. But it was the CW which, in 2014, really put the actress in the spotlight, thanks to their flagship series: The 100 . She plays Doctor Abigail Griffin, Clarke’s mother and Chancellor Jaha’s right-hand woman, a key character who gains popularity over the episodes.
