Alexa Ray Joel Described Her Dad Billy Joel 'A Real Mensch'

 Posted on May 15, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  705 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

William Martin Joel aka Billy Joel has three children, namely, Alexa Ray Joel, Della Rose Joel, and Remy Anne Joel, and he encloses a close bond with all his daughters. They have some undeniable father-daughter-love, which is clear to every nooks and corner of the world.

Recently, one of Billy Joel's daughter, Alexa Ray Joel made some comments regarding her dad, where she described him as 'A Real Mensch.' She commented on Billy Joel's habit of gifting tickets in the front row to those in the nosebleeds, she and her mom, Christie Brinkley commented 'A Real Mensch!' using a Yiddish word, which means a 'good person.'

The comment is also a description of how Billy Joel' never sells tickets to the front row of his concerts.' The post follows a comment that cites:

"He got tired of all the bored, rich people staring up at him — so now, he sends his road crew out to bring down the fans from the worst seats so there'll be 'people in the front row that are really happy to be there, real fans."

The post features the 2015 Billboard cover story where Joel taled about the good spirit and his distaste for the scalpers. 'We never sell front rows, we hold those tickets at just about every concert,' he told the magazine at the time. 'For years, the scalpers got the tickets and would scalp the front row for ridiculous amounts of money,' the post says.

Alexa Ray Joel further mentioned that her dad got sick on the rich people sitting in the front row of his show without making noise as if he was not performing. 'It turns out the real fans were always in the back of the room in the worst seats,' she added.

Joel further wrote:

"We now hold those tickets, and I send my road crew out to the back of the room when the audience comes in and they get people from the worst seats and bring 'em into the front rows. This way you've got people in the front row that are really happy to be there, real fans."
