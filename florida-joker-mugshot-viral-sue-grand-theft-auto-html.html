    A Florida man is demanding money from Rockstar Games after he says the company used his likeness in their new game, Grand Theft Auto VI. 

    Lawrence Sullivan's mugshot first went viral after his 2017 arrest, when the public noted similarities between his intense face tattoos and green hair and DC Comics' villain, The Joker. 

    Widely known as the Florida Joker, Sullivan is now threatening to sue the New York-based video game company for featuring a character allegedly created in his own likeness.

    Rockstar Games released a 90-second trailer for its long-awaited sixth edition of Grand Theft Auto last week, featuring many real-life incidents that took place in Florida since GTA V hit the market. 

    Lawrence Sullivan's 2017 mugshot went viral after the public noted similarities between his intense face tattoos and green hair and DC Comics' villain, The Joker

    At one point in the trailer, viewers can see a man with facial tattoos and purple-colored hair wearing a prison uniform against a Sheriff's Office emblem. 

    The Florida Joker soon released a TikTok video claiming that the company had copied his look and asked for nearly two million dollars. 

    'You might have seen that character on GTA 6, [they] just dropped the trailer today,' says Sullivan.

    'You know, they got that character with the face tattoos, you know who they got that inspired by. By me! Just look me up: Florida Joker, Miami Joker, you know what I'm talking about. GTA, we gotta talk.' 

    'We gotta talk or [if] not you gotta give me like a mil or two,' Sullivan said in another TikTok video. 'Florida Joker ain't having that, y'all took my likeness, y'all took my life.'

    Since then, he has released multiple pictures comparing himself and the character's look and dyed his hair purple.

    Miami-Dade police arrested him for aiming a gun at passing vehicles in 2017. 

    When officers spoke to him, Sullivan told them he had a loaded gun in his pocket without a concealed weapons permit, police said. He told the police that the permit was 'expensive'. 

    His mugshot went viral when he violated his probation from a charge of carrying a concealed firearm from the 2017 arrest, according to the Pinellas County Sheriff's Office. 

    Jail records listed multiple tattoos, including brass knuckles, 666 (also known as the 'number of the beast'), a sword, a skull, the devil and the grim reaper, a giant bat tattoo with what appears to be a vulgar message directed at DC Comics superhero, The Batman. 

    Since he went viral for trying to sue Rockstar Games, Roger Clark, the actor behind Red Dead Redemption's character Arthur Morgan, also took to TikTok to comment on the controversy. 

     '[Rockstar Games is] lawyered up, man. They know exactly what they can and cannot get away with. If I were you, I would use the notoriety they just threw your way to your advantage. Capitalize on it somehow. You ain’t getting a job at Home Depot with that face,' he said.

    In 2014, Lindsay Lohan hit Take-Two Interactive , Rockstar's parent company, with a civil lawsuit saying they used her image for a character named Lacey Jonas without her permission in GTA V

    This is not the first time Rockstar Games has faced such allegations. In 2014, Lindsay Lohan hit Take-Two Interactive, Rockstar's parent company, with a civil lawsuit saying they used her image for a character named Lacey Jonas without her permission in GTA V .

    The legal papers claimed that the 'game featured a mission where the plaintiff's look-alike asks another character to take her home and escape the paparazzi' and showed a character sporting the same ' likeness, clothing, outfits, clothing line products, ensemble in the form of hats, hairstyle, sunglasses, jean shorts worn by [Lohan]...before the release of the GTA V video game.'

    The video game cover also strikingly resembled a famous picture of Lohan in a swimsuit, taking a selfie and making a peace sign.

    But in 2016, Lohan lost the lawsuit. The judges ruled in Take-Two's favor, dismissing her case and ruling that the game was not using her likeness.

    Rockstar Games has not yet responded to DailyMail.com's request for a comment
