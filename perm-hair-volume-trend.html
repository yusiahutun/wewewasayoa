    Is the perm the biggest most surprising hair trend for 2020?

    The perm is back: there aren't many other four word phrases in beauty that could cause such a flicker of panic in even the most hardy of hair trend followers. But for 2020, a softer, more modern approach to the permanent wave might be the solution you've been searching for to add volume and movement

    07 May 2024, 00:00

    The perm is back: there aren't many other four word phrases in beauty that could cause such a flicker of panic in even the most hardy of hair trend followers.

    But for 2020, a softer, more modern approach to the permanent wave might be the solution you've been searching for to add volume and movement to your tresses.

    The history of the perm

    In truth, the perm has never completely disappeared, but of all the hair trends of the last few decades, the perm is one of the most contentious.

    At the height of its popularity in the '80s, the perm was everywhere, from Jennifer Gray in Dirty Dancing to Meg Ryan in When Harry Met Sally; nobody was putting the hairstyle in a corner.

    Among the ordinary women flocking to the salon to get tight curls of their own, was my mother, whose naturally poker-straight hair I also inherited.

    "When I first had a perm, they were very popular - almost everyone had one - and the tight curls were a way of giving body and greater styling options to my really fine hair", she says. "I liked it, but I stopped because the tight curl pattern was difficult to manage and style, especially as it grew out, and without using the harsh chemicals my hair is in much better condition."

    Becki Murray

    So, as with many hair trends, the perm fell out-of-favour, leaving me to grow up in the noughties, desperately searching for ways to enliven my own 'curtain tresses' without the thought of getting one even crossing my mind.

    The modern perm

    Things, though, are changing.

    First and foremost, you may have noticed that actresses have slowly fallen back in love with the perm, including Emma Stone and Olivia Munn.

    The hairstyle even made it onto the spring/summer 2020 Fashion Week catwalk at the Julien Macdonald show.

    Related Story

    The perm process has also been undergoing a low-profile transformation. There are more techniques being used to limit damage - both from the chemicals and the heat required to set the curls - as well as more than one finished look. There's still the option of big, corkscrew curls, but looser, beachier waves are also now possible too.

    "In our salon, perms have been on the rise for about five years, and recently we’ve gone from doing about four a year to four a day", says the hair expert Karine Jackson who uses Curlformers at her location in London's Seven Dials.

    "We see a real variety in what clients’ want – some want really defined curls, others just a bit more movement and wave", Jackson continues. "On the whole, the modern perm is much softer and gentler now; I think the word perm brings up connotations of that super curly old-style perm but technology (and taste!) has moved on."

    A first-hand experience

    Deciding to take the plunge myself, indirectly mirroring my mother's own trip to the salon, my journey began at the One2One salon in Mayfair, just down the road from where, rumour has it, the first hair stylist to bring perming to the UK was based.

    There I met Christian Toth, a leading expert in hair shaping who launched the Digital Perm into Britain. This method of perming (which looks a bit like you're undergoing an alien experiment), keeps the heat-shaping process at a constant temperature, limiting additional heat damage.

    Becki Murray for Harper's Bazaar

    In combination with the use of curling tools (instead of traditional rods), there's also a lot less room for error, none of the hook ends that caused a lot of frizz in the '80s and you don't have to wait for the perm to 'drop'. Instead, you walk out of the salon with your curls complete.

    And, I loved it. The finished result was a very gentle wave that enabled me to actually keep a curl in my hair throughout the working day (not just for an hour or two). Damage-wise, the ends are a little drier and knot more easily, but I haven't lost any shine, probably because the solution was only applied from below my ears (where you would also start using a curling tong for loose, beachy waves).

    Key advice

    1. It's not for everyone

    While techniques now exist to allow somewhat damaged and lightened hair to be permed, it's not recommended. "I would not recommend carrying out a perm on very fine, limp hair, as it will not hold onto the new structure", explains Toth. "Your hair should also be in even condition from root to end." That might mean you need to cut your hair or avoid bleaching it for an effective finish.

    2. Bring photos

    Before getting a perm, you'll have a consultation to check that your hair is suitable for the treatment and to discuss the curl finish you want. Toth's advice: "bring photos of when you last absolutely loved how your hair looked when wavy." That means your stylist can choose the rod size that will get you closest to your desired look.

    3. It takes time

    You can expect to spend three to four hours at the salon and there's a lot of waiting around, as both the initial processing solution and the actual perming process takes time to work its magic.

    4. Aftercare is really important

    You have to wait at least 48 hours before washing your hair to avoid ruining the curls and, when you do, you'll likely need to invest in a new haircare routine. "We always recommend that products should be high in protein and low in moisture content to maintain good result" says Toth. "Using a diffuser when blow-drying, also helps activate the curls, which will maintain the look for longer."

    5. You still have to style it

    Speaking of a diffuser, that's the main sticking point for the modern perm - it still takes styling. You might dream of rolling out of bed with perfect waves, but that's unlikely unless you choose a very tight curl pattern. For beachy waves, your diffuser and a little bit of curl defining cream are your best friends. But, when styled, you'll have perfect waves that stay in place for longer.

    Final thoughts

    During the process, I met a woman who remembered getting her first perm back in the '80s, telling me with such excitement about going to the salon together with her girlfriends.

    I think that's the secret to the style. Just like a dramatic haircut or change of colour, getting a perm is the perfect opportunity to experiment. Plus, with new techniques available to limit damage and give you more control over the finish you want, it's increasingly a more viable option for increasingly volume and movement.

    The perm is back. Long live the perm...
