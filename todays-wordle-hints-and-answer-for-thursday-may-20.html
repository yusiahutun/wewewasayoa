How Does Wordle Work?

Wordle is a daily word-guessing game where you have six tries to get the five-letter word of the day. For each attempt, players will be given certain clues that will help them guess the Wordle.

If a player guesses a word and they are given a green box around one letter they put in, that means the letter is in the Wordle of the day and it is in the correct position within the word. If a player guesses a word and one of their letters has a yellow box around them, that means that the letter is in the day’s word but not in the correct position. Last, if a player guesses a word and a letter in the word has a gray box around it, that means that the letter is not in the word at all.

There are plenty of other alternative games that Wordle players can choose from if they are unsatisfied with just one Wordle per day.

You can play Wordle for free via the New York Times’ website on a smart device or PC.
