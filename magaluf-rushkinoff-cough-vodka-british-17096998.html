    Have you got the Magaluf cough? Brits abroad getting 'throat killing' illness

    Rushkinoff is the Spanish brand that's sold in most bars and restaurants at the resort because it's mega cheap at only 5 a bottle. But holidaymakers are reporting coming down with the Rushkinoff Cough after enjoying a couple of boozy bevvies leaving them hacking and rasping.

    18 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Rushkinoff is the Spanish brand that's sold in most bars and restaurants at the resort because it's mega cheap at only £5 a bottle.

    But holidaymakers are reporting coming down with the Rushkinoff Cough after enjoying a couple of boozy bevvies – leaving them hacking and rasping.

    Recently, the Magaluf 2016 Twitter page asked its followers: "Who's looking forward to the Rushkinoff Cough this summer?"

    Numerous people have also used social media to complain about how horrific the effects of the vodka are.

    One Brit girl Tweeted: "The Ruskinoff Cough is slowly killin me (sic)".

    On the Facebook page dedicated to those that have come down with it, one person wrote: “Avoid it if at all possible! By the end of the holiday I had stopped buying vodka drinks when out because my throat couldn’t take it. I lost my voice... I spent a week at home with a wicked cough and I could hardly speak.”

    But a spokesman for the brand said its all down to the fact boozy Brits drink so much of the stuff.

    He said: “This is the problem – the amount the British drink. In other areas in the island where German people go, Scandinavian, Italians, Spanish, there is not the same problem – the problem is the British and how much they drink.

    “We have news in the papers every day about the uncivilised behaviour of the Brits here, we don’t hear the same about other tourists visiting Mallorca.”

    Dr Matt Oldfield, from Kingston Hospital, London, backed up the theory saying drinking heavily causes a severely weakened immune system that's not able to fight infections.

    He added: "Staying up late and drinking to excess is going to make it harder to resist infections but the infection is probably being passed on by close contact with lots of other people you wouldn't normally be so close to."
