    Everything you need to know about Virtue haircare

    In our new regular feature #TheBrand, Bazaars digital beauty director looks into an exciting and efficacious brand taking the beauty industry by storm. This month its Virtue, haircare that uses a patented protein ethically sourced from human hair.

    06 May 2024, 00:00

    In our new regular feature #TheBrand, Bazaar’s digital beauty director looks into an exciting and efficacious brand taking the beauty industry by storm. This month it’s Virtue, haircare that uses a patented protein ethically sourced from human hair.

    What’s the story?

    If you’re forever looking for hair repair in a bottle, call off the search. US-export Virtue haircare harnesses a transformative protein in each of its treatment and styling products.

    Discovered by an ex-army colonel using keratin for wound healing and tissue regrowth, he unlocked a way to extract wonder ingredient Alpha Keratin 60ku™ which was found to also repair hair. They patented the protein for consumer use and Virtue was born.

    “Alpha Keratin 60ku™is a pure, whole, ethically sourced human keratin protein,” explains Adir Abergel, Virtue’s creative director. “It’s molecule is the same exact size as our own, making it instantly recognisable by our bodies, working to seamlessly fill in and repair any damage in each strand of hair.” The brand says it’s clinically shown to restore the structural integrity. Good news for brittle, damaged and over-processed hair.

    Who endorses it?

    Abergel happens to be one of the biggest hair players in Hollywood. Jessica Biel’s romantic waves at the Emmys, Saoirse Ronan’s sleek bob at the Oscars, Anne Hathaway’s spiked-halo hairstyle at the Met Ball – you name it he did it – and not just now, he’s been behind red carpet hairstyles for the last 20 years.

    By using these products on his A-list clientele, Abergel’s job becomes easier and easier. Thanks to him, “Saoirse Ronan, Jennifer Garner, Anne Hathaway, Jessica Biel and so many more have Virtue in their showers,” he tells Bazaar.

    From La to London, here the queen of blondes, colourist Nicola Clarke who works with the likes of Cate Blanchett, Madonna, Kate Moss and more, has brought it into her salon Nicola Clarke at John Frieda on Margaret Street. Try it there or buy it at from Cult Beauty from today.

    Why else is it special?

    The human keratin is the real game-changer, but Virtue has other attributes. The formulas are free of sulfates, parabens, colours and dyes, plus they’re not heavy on fragrance.

    “The scents in our products are so light and beautiful and don’t overwhelm or interfere with any other scent you might be wearing,” Abergel adds. We can concur.

    “The formulas are also incredibly lightweight, you can touch your hair all day without feeling the product.” Reassuring, considering 'hair repair' products can often feel heavy on fine strands, and weigh down thick hair even more.

    Where should we start?

    Naturally, you’re likely to see the biggest benefits in terms of split-end repair from the shampoos and conditioners, (£14 each, there are three of each suited to different hair types) and the Restorative Treatment Mask, £26, but the styling products are really efficacious too.

    Abergel’s top pick? “The Moisture-Defining Whip, £17, is my favourite at the moment because it’s extremely versatile and easy for people to use.” Hair hack alert: “You can use it easily to air-dry or diffuse hair to enhance natural texture and bring out waves. I also like to use it on set to break up the hair after curling. I apply a golf ball size amount into my hands and scrunch it into hair to enhance the style.”

    We also love the One For All 6-In-One Styler, £17, which primes, softens, serves heat protection – and the rest.
