    Early Life and Education

    Martin Short was born on March 26, 1950, in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada. He attended Westdale Secondary School and McMaster University.

    Rise to Fame on “SCTV”

    Martin Short gained recognition for his comedic genius on the sketch comedy television series “SCTV,” where he portrayed memorable characters such as Ed Grimley and Jackie Rogers Jr.

    Breakthrough on “Saturday Night Live”

    Short’s career skyrocketed when he became a cast member on “Saturday Night Live” in His vibrant characters and hilarious impressions endeared him to audiences worldwide.

    The Iconic Role of Franck Eggelhoffer

    Short’s portrayal of the eccentric wedding planner Franck Eggelhoffer in the film “Father of the Bride” and its sequel solidified his status as a comedy legend.

    Award-Winning Broadway Performer

    In addition to his screen work, Short has earned critical acclaim for his performances on Broadway, winning a Tony Award for his one-man show, “Martin Short: Fame Becomes Me.”

    Voice Acting Extraordinaire

    Short has lent his voice to various animated films, including “The Prince of Egypt,” “Madagascar,” and “The Secret Life of Pets.” His distinct voice brings characters to life with charm and humor.

    A Star on Canada’s Walk of Fame

    In recognition of his contributions to the entertainment industry, Martin Short was honored with a star on Canada’s Walk of Fame in 2000.

    The Love for Music and Singing

    Besides acting, Short is also an accomplished singer and entertainer. He has showcased his musical talents in various performances and even released his own album, “I Must Say: My Life As a Humble Comedy Legend.”

    Collaborations with Steve Martin

    Martin Short’s comedic chemistry with fellow actor and comedian Steve Martin is legendary. The duo has performed together in several comedy tours and even co-hosted the Academy Awards in 2010.

    A Generous Philanthropist

    Martin Short is known for his philanthropic efforts, supporting causes such as cancer research, children’s hospitals, and educational programs.

    A Multilingual Comedian

    One of Short’s unique talents is his ability to seamlessly switch between accents and languages, adding an extra layer of humor to his performances.

    The Voice of The Cat in the Hat

    Short’s vocal talents shine through in the animated television series “The Cat in the Hat Knows a Lot About That!” where he provides the voice for the mischievous cat.

    Going Behind the Camera

    The Role of Jiminy Glick

    Short’s portrayal of the overweight and brash interviewer Jiminy Glick is a testament to his willingness to push boundaries and create hilarious and unforgettable characters.

    A Consistent Guest on Late Night Shows

    Known for his witty banter and comedic charm, Martin Short has been a frequent guest on various late-night talk shows, including “The Tonight Show” and “Late Night with David Letterman.”

    Emmy and Grammy Nominations

    His exceptional talent has earned Short numerous award nominations, including Emmy nominations for his work on “SCTV” and a Grammy nomination for his comedy album “I Must Say.”
