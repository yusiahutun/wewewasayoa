    Uhuru angers Kenyans after confessing over KSh 2 billion is stolen daily in his gov't

    President Uhuru Kenyatta has left Kenyans with a bad taste in their mouths after blatantly stating that KSh 2 billion is stolen every single day in his government.

    The president, who has, time and again, reiterated his commitment to fighting graft in the Jubilee government outrightly confessed that the country was losing over KSh 2 billion daily to corruption.

    While defending the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) and the humongous referendum budget, Uhuru said though the cost of the plebiscite was yet to be determined, it would definitely be less than what his government loses to corruption.

    "We are yet to know how much the BBI referendum will cost because we are yet to reach that stage. The money they steal daily is over the said KSh 2 billion of the referendum. "These people (anti-BBI) are disingenuous. Let us focus on what will protect you and your children. I am not really forcing you to be with me but do not trust anyone blindly," he said.

    The president was speaking in an interview on various Kikuyu radio stations on Monday, January 18.

    However, his utterances left tongues wagging with many questioning what he was doing about the alleged graft.

    Kuira Brian said:

    "I like how in Kenya we talk about 2 Billion shillings like its 20 bob."

    Mihr Thakar said:

    "No, Mr President. I cannot allow people to eat Kshs. 2 billion each day while paying MORE THAN 50% of my income in taxes. If that amount of theft (over Kshs. 700 billion a year) was extinguished, Kenya could soon have a budget surplus!"

    Besides the BBI, the head of state also subtly delved into the 2022 succession politics and said that was not his priority.

    “I am saddened by politicians who have become too selfish and are only concerned with 2022 politics. That time is yet to come. When it comes to elections, Kenyans will vote for whoever they want. And we will be alert. We will get the appropriate person to lead the country,” he said.

    He said he would first concentrate on fulfilling all the pre-election promises he made to Kenyans and then later turn his focus to politics.
