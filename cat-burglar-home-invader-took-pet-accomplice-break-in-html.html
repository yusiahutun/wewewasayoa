    Cat burglar: Home invader took pet accomplice on break in

    A home invader brought new meaning to the term 'cat burglar' when he brought his feline pet named 'Spaghetti' into an Oregon home and then proceeded to try on the homeowner's clothes and help himself to coffee and cake.

    18 Jul 2024, 00:00

    A home invader brought new meaning to the term 'cat burglar' when he brought his feline pet named 'Spaghetti' into an Oregon home and then proceeded to try on the homeowner's clothes and help himself to coffee and cake. 

    Ryan Bishop, 38, of Gresham, was found in the house, some 15 miles east of Portland, having stolen the homeowner's Christmas onesie and made himself a coffee while snacking on some cupcakes that had been left out.

    When Timothy Smith and his fiancée returned to their property, they noticed damage to their kitchen and items that didn't belong to them.

    Ryan Bishop, 38, of Gresham, was bizarrely found in the house, some 15 miles east of Portland, having stolen the homeowner's Christmas onesie and made himself a coffee while snacking on some cupcakes that had been left out

    'So I grab two of the knives and I'm walking around looking for someone because clearly a person broke in,' Smith, 24, told The Oregonian on Monday. 

    Smith saw that a hatch to a crawl space inside a third bedroom was open, so he went down there with the knifes. 

    He crawled through the roughly 3-feet tall space and yelled for anyone inside to announce themselves.

    That's when they said they found a cat wearing a shirt in the crawl space beneath the house, Fox 12 reported.   

    When Portland police arrived, a man wearing the owner's Christmas onesie also emerged from the crawl space, the report said.  

    'No one leaves a dressed cat in a crawl space unless they're coming back or they're still here,' Smith said. 'So I got out and shut the door' he added.

    It then emerged that Bishop took his fiancee's Christmas onesie — 'like a zip-up, it's got little snowball things that bounce off of it' — and put it on. 

    Smith also told reporters: 'He [Bishop] pulled the cupcakes out of the fridge, ate a few of those, pulled the cake out, ate a piece of cake, just made himself a cup of coffee. 

    'He was just hanging out wearing my fiancee's onesie, like, doped up and drinking coffee,' Smith said. 

    When Portland police arrived, a man wearing the owner's Christmas onesie emerged from the crawl space, the report said.

    A furious Smith continued: 'We were livid. Our sanctuary was destroyed by someone who had no business being there and here he is wearing my fiancee's clothes. 

    'There's just no reason for it.'  

    Bishop was booked in the Multnomah County Jail on first-degree burglary, first-degree criminal mischief, and third-degree theft,. 

    Animal control was called to the scene after Spaghetti refused to come out of the crawl space. Police said the homeowner declined her onesie not be returned after the suspect was arrested.
