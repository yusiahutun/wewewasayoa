    In the world of clean skincare, Tata Harper remains the blueprint. Started by its namesake founder in 2010 on an organic farm in Vermont, the brand is known for its elegant, luxe, aeffective botanical formulas. And to introduce its first-ever hand cream (an already luxurious product elevated with Harper’s beautiful formulas and packaging), Tata Harper teamed up with Sky High Farm, a nonprofit organization in Hudson Valley, New York that focuses on combating food insecurity.

    Founded by artist Dan Colen, Sky High Farm is no stranger to collaborations. Its commercial offshoot Sky High Farm Universe—cofounded and run by CEO Daphne Seybold, formerly of Comme des Garçons and Dover Street Market—recently partnered with Balenciaga to create farm-inspired, designer workwear and even released a workwear capsule of its own with fashion industry creatives, including our very own editor in chief, Samira Nasr. But for its partnership with Tata Harper, Sky High worked with the skincare brand to create a moisturizing hand cream that hydrates and nourishes—an ode to the many ways in which the hands are our ultimate interface with the rest of the world, and how they signify strength, love, and labor, as the brand explained in a press release.

    Like Tata Harper’s existing skincare and makeup launches, the Nourishing Hand Cream (out today) features the brand’s botanic technology and 26 ethically sourced ingredients. In the rich cream, you can find upcycled strawberry seed oil to smooth skin and promote cell regeneration, six butters to moisturize deeply, and oat ceramides to support barrier function and soften skin.

    Tata Harper x Sky High Farm Nourishing Hand Cream

    Tata Harper x Sky High Farm Nourishing Hand Cream

    Even the packaging, which features colorful fruits, vegetables, and flowers, is an ode to the two brands’ botanical backgrounds. Plus, the rich cream boasts a pastel-pink hue and a light scent of lavender, Roman chamomile, and lemon myrtle.

    Related Stories

    While the hydration and smoothing benefits are enough reason alone to snag this hand cream, Tata Harper will also be donating 100 percent of the proceeds to Sky High Farm to further the organization’s mission to keep the hungry fed.

    The Nourishing Hand Cream is available today for $50 at tataharperskincare.com.
