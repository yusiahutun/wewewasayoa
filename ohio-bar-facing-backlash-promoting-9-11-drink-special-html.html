    Ohio bar facing backlash after promoting a 9/11 drink special

    27 May 2024, 00:00

    A hookah bar in Ohio sparked controversy after it promoted a drink a 9/11 drink special on one of its social media accounts on September 11th. 

    Karma Kafe in Akron shared an image to its Instagram page showing a shot glass containing red and blue liquor. 

    According to ABC Cleveland, the original caption beneath the photograph read: 'Today's special, the 9/11 shot only $4!!! Because why not capitalize on something that happened 18 years ago.'

    The post immediately prompted outrage, and the text beneath the image has now been edited to read: 'Today's special, the 9/11 shot only $4.00!!!!'

    Some 2,977 victims were killed during the terrorist attacks against the United States on September 11, 2001.

    Another member of the public wrote: 'This post just got featured in the 'Marketing Fails' Facebook group'.

    The Karma Kafe account shamelessly replied: 'Haha no way?!? That's awesome!!!! 

    They then added the quotations: 'All publicity is good publicity, as long as they spell your name right.' 'The only thing worse than being talked about is not being talked about.' 'There's no such thing as bad publicity except your own obituary.'

    The person behind the social media account claimed business at Karma Kafe has been booming after the controversial post.

    However, the comments left on the site have been less than kind.
