    In an age where youth is a prized possession and timeless looking celebrities adorn magazine covers, women are constantly on the hunt for the next secret elixir of youth. 

    It's no secret that many women are tempted by filler - and countless celebrities have had a helping hand - but often ladies feel intimated at the prospect of it looking too obvious.

    So how obvious is it and do you think you can spot a lady who has had a treatment from one who hasn't? We've pit three ladies - one who has had filler for ten years, one who has had just one treatment and one who has never had it - against each other to find out. So can you tell who is who? 

    We've pit three ladies - one who has had filler for ten years, one who has had just one treatment and one who has never had it - against each other to find out. So can you tell who is who? L-R: Kate Baines, a 50-year-old account manager from London, has never had filler, Tracie Green, 50, who is self-employed and from Manchester, has had dermal filler once - a decision she made after losing a lot of weight very quickly and Louise James, 50, a makeup artist from the West Midlands, has been having filler for ten years - and is delighted with the results

    'I'VE BEEN HAVING FILLER FOR TEN YEARS'   

    Louise James, 50, a makeup artist from the West Midlands, has been having filler for ten years - and is delighted with the results. 

    Louise, who first had treatment when she was 35 after becoming interested in ageing preventative options, said: 'I always wanted to achieve natural looking results and nothing fake, so knew I had to find a good practitioner.'

    Louise turned to Miss Jonquille Chantrey, Cosmetic Surgeon, Expert Aesthetics, who she said talked her through all the benefits of preventative treatments and was really clear about how it can assist in the ageing process. 

    Louise James, 50, a makeup artist from the West Midlands, has been having filler for ten years - and is delighted with the results. Louise, left, before her treatment, and, right, after, says that people have commented on how well she looks, which is the result she wanted

    'I think the treatment has definitely made a difference to me,' said Louise. 'People have commented that I look well and those that know about the treatment have always liked the results.'

    Speaking about her treatment, Miss Jonquille Chantrey said: 'Like many ladies, Louise was starting to look slightly tired around her eyes, eyebrows and temples, as well as developing forehead lines. She was also losing support from around her chin and the lip area.

    'I advised she had Juvéderm filler in her temple, forehead, eyebrow and cheek to stop further drooping of these areas. Also, by treating her cheek and chin, it stopped her jowl from sagging further at the jawline. 

    'By gently re-creating her lip shape this stopped her lip from thinning – another sign of ageing. Subtle injections into the cheek would be a common preventative treatment to receive over a ten-year period - as long as it is not overdone. Medical skincare containing retinols and antioxidants are also very important to achieve a smoother skin and bright complexion.'

    'I'VE HAD FILLER JUST ONCE'  

    Tracie Green, 50, who is self-employed and from Manchester, has had dermal filler once - a decision she made after losing a lot of weight very quickly.  

    She said: 'I decided to have treatment as I lost a lot of weight very quickly and I felt this aged my face. I originally went in to the consultation concerned about certain areas that I thought they would treat, however, Miss Chantrey looked at my face as a whole and explained that treating these areas would not get me the results I wanted. 

    'She advised treating other areas of the face, which then had an impact on my areas of concern and made my face look much better overall.'

    Tracie Green, 50, who is self-employed and from Manchester, has had dermal filler once - a decision she made after losing a lot of weight very quickly. Tracie, left, before, and, right, after, was given injections to lift and re-shape the face after the weightloss

    Tracie says that when she got home after having her treatment, her children and closest family and friends noticed a 'big improvement', whilst other friends who didn't know she had been for treatment commented on how 'well' she was looking. 

    'The results were very natural, which was what I wanted - I did not want a false look or a look that was obvious to others that I had had treatment. I wanted an improvement,' she added.

    Explaining how she treated Tracie after her significant weightloss, Miss Jonquille Chantrey said: 'Tracie was a very interesting case as she had lost a significant amount of weight quickly. Her areas of concern were her nose-to-mouth lines and her lips. 

    'I carefully explained all the areas in the face that had lost support with the fat loss and the impact this had on her skin quality, folds and overall face shape. She had lost fat from her temples, cheeks, under eyes, jawline and chin. 

    'I treated her over one session in a lecture to other doctors using highly advanced Juvéderm injections to lift and re-shape the face. All of the mentioned areas were injected. Interestingly, I did not inject her lip or nose-to-mouth lines. Instead, I indirectly lifted her other surrounding tissues, which took the shadows away and allowed her natural lip shape to be revealed.'

    'I'VE NEVER HAD A TREATMENT' 

    Unlike the other ladies, Kate Baines, a 50-year-old account manager from London, has never had filler.  

    Kate Baines, a 50-year-old account manager from London, has never had filler but is considering trying it out

    She is, however, tempted to consider having a little work done to boost her self confidence.

    After meeting with Kate to examine her face, Miss Jonquille Chantrey said: 'It’s important a lady like Kate, who has had no treatment at all, sees a medical professional with significant experience in aesthetics in order to gently enhance her and keep her looking appropriate for her age.'

    Miss Chantrey's suggested treatment plan for her was medical skincare followed by Juvéderm injections to hydrate and boost her skin smoothness.

    'When dealing with a new patient, I start with their current diagnosis – where have they lost specific fat from, how are their facial bones changing and what is their skin quality like? If they present to me in their 20’s with minimal ageing I tend to advise on good skin practice rather than other interventions. I believe in working with good skin as the main preventer and also healthy lifestyle choices. 

    'If more severe ageing is already present then, of course, more work will be needed to correct and then prevent further changes. I explain their options for treatment to achieve their goals on an improvement scale, starting with no intervention up to significant correction.   

    'In the long-term, early treatment of facial structure can slow the process of ageing in other areas, which in turn helps the patient spend less.'
