    It happens to all of us: No matter how well-intentioned we are in getting a homemade dinner on the table, days just get so hectic that our plans often go poof. That’s why we’re big fans of picking up packages of thinly sliced chicken breast at the grocery store. Not only does it cut down on prep time, those pre-sliced pieces cook up super-fast — it’s a win-win! And fast doesn’t have to mean flavorless, thanks to these shortcut recipes from the First for Women test kitchen that’ll have a delicious dinner ready in as little as 25 minutes. Just keep scrolling for our favorite thin sliced chicken breast recipes.

    7 weeknight-easy thin sliced chicken breast recipes

    Buying chicken breasts that are already sliced makes for quick work in the kitchen. Note that the USDA recommends using raw chicken within 1 to 2 days of purchase. “I always store uncooked chicken on the bottom shelf in the refrigerator,” says First for Women test kitchen manager Susan Chiusano. “That’s the coldest spot in the fridge, which helps the meat stay fresh longer.” If you won’t be able to use the chicken within that time frame, it’s best to stash it in the freezer, where it will last about 9 months.
