    • His father was an influential Moi-era politician, with the soft-spoken prime cabinet secretary nominee estimating his wealth to be around KSh 4 billion
    • Like many other politicians with old money, Mudavadi owns numerous stately homes in Karen and Riverside, Nairobi

    Musalia Mudavadi announced his net worth to be KSh 4 billion while being vetted for the prime cabinet secretary post in Parliament.

    Mudavadi's huge Karen mansion

    Mudavadi occupied a mansion in Riverside until his unique Karen residence was completed, and images of the sprawling mansion surfaced when his mother passed away.

    Politicians visiting to commiserate with Mudavadi offered a glimpse of the luxurious life he leads.

    The house sits on an expansive piece of land and has two main wings, two storeys high, connected by a one-storey section between them. The buildings have huge, Mediterranean-style windows on all levels.

    Mudavadi’s huge compound boasting a well-manicured lawn, can host hundreds of people, as was the case when his mother passed away.

    The walls are of pale cream and brown, with parts of the compound paved using cabro blocks arranged in a circular pattern and a circular driveway dots the front of the main house.

    Security is a priority for the politician, and a perimeter wall with climbing plants surrounds the compound. It is topped off with barbed wire to keep off intruders.

    Workers die at Mudavadi’s house

    In 2019, the mansion made headlines for the wrong reasons during its construction when three people died at the site.

    The ANC leader owned up to his association with the site but claimed he was a project partner.
