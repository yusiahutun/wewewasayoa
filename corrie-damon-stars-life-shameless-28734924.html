    Coronation Street actor Ciaran Griffiths made his mark in the acting world from a very young age and fans may recognise him from a string of appearances on popular TV hits.

    Having starring in children’s drama series Children’s Ward at just 13 years old, the star also appeared in numerous soaps and television series such as City Road, The Bill, Holby City, Waterloo Road, and EastEnders.

    But Ciarán is perhaps best known for playing gay gangster Mickey Maguire in the comedy drama Shameless, which he had starred in from 2007 to 2012.

    Aside from soap appearances, Ciaran made a name for himself when he appeared in the hit British comedy drama Shameless.

    The 39-year-old starred as troublemaker Micky Maguire, who was often seen being violent, aggressive and mean.

    His character had a pivotal role in the series when he was seen trying to come to terms with his sexuality as a secretly gay man.

    He departed the popular show in series nine in dramatic fashion after he paid a visit to a drug dealer that owed his mum money.

    After being trapped by the drug dealers dog, he found himself locked in a room the with the drug dealers wife as she went into labour.

    Despite his best attempts to help, the mother died and Micky took the baby girl and started a new life with her as his own child.

    Heckled by fans

    Actor Ciaran became known for playing gay gangster Micky Maguire on British comedy drama Shameless but the role led to some unfortunate heckling from trolls as a result of his role.

    Ciaran, who is straight in real life, says he is regularly heckled and mocked for playing a gay ­bad boy on the show, with boozed-up lads often trying to pick fights with him.

    The actor claims it even prevents him from chatting to girls when he’s out on the town with his mates.

    He revealed: “It’s usually a bunch of drunk lads.

    “They know I am not gay and then one lad will say, ‘I have to ask this question’ and he’ll get all his mates’ attention and say, ‘Are you gay?’

    “They all fall about laughing but I generally reply, ‘Ask your dad!’

    “I did that once and ended up having to leave the club.”

    He admitted even potential girlfriends ask him, but it hasn’t affected his success in that department.

    “I don’t mind it when girls ask the question and I do all right for ­girlfriends.”

    One run-in in particular when he was confronted by some builders in his local area.

    “I once had a run-in with a few ­builders who were working near me.

    “They asked if I was the guy off Shameless and when I said yes they started up, ‘The gay one, the gay one’.

    "They were all from Liverpool and ­giving me stick. I was going to go back and sort them out but thought better of it.”

    Eagle-eyed fans may remember Ciaran from a forgotten appearance in Corrie over two decades ago as a different character.

    Way back in 2000, he had already guest starred as Dean Sykes in four episodes.

    Co-star bond

    Damon has forced his son Jacob to leave Weatherfield since arriving on Coronation Street.

    After refusing to be part of his drug dealing business, Damon battered Jacob and instrcuted him to leave or his girlfriend Amy Barlow's (Elle Mulvaney) life would be at risk.
