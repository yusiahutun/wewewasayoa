    Tyler Florence: Career

    In 1998, Florence decided to spread his wings on the chef world on his own, becoming executive chef of Cibo. He eventually opened up Cafeteria in Manhattan's up-tone Chelsea neighborhood. It worked wonders for the chef and led to various guest appearances on a variety of shows on the Food Network. A year later, Florence was signed to host the cable network's Food 911 and he also hosted How to Boil Water, Tyler's Ultimate, The Great Food Truck Race and various other Food Network specials.

    Florence owns his own chain of restaurants

    Source: Daily Herald

    People magazine titled Florence the "sexiest chef alive" in 2003. Florence also prides himself on being an all-American cook who has his term with simple flavors and fresh ingredients. Tyler's Ultimate: Brilliant Simple Food to Make Any Time, his 2006 book, encourages people to make every meal a worthwhile occasion with friends and family.  He opened his first kitchen, The Tyler Florence Shop retail shop in 2008, and published the bestselling books Stirring the Pot and Dinner at My Place.

    In June 2010, Florence opened the first signature restaurant Wayfare Tavern, in San Francisco's Financial District to his name. That same year with the experience collected and networks made through his restaurant, he collaborated with musician Sammy Hagar and opened Rotisserie & Wine in Napa and El Paseo in Mill Valley. The handsome chef owns kitchen retail stores, Tyler Florence Shop and also co-owns a line of organic baby and toddler food, Sprout.

    Tyler Florence has 11 published cookbooks, over 4 hit TV shows and three upscale restaurants in prime locations. Needless to say that he is one of the most successful and acclaimed chefs out there.
