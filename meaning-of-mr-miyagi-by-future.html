    Meaning of Mr. Miyagi by Future

    03 May 2024, 00:00

    Future's song "Mr. Miyagi" encompasses a theme of empowerment and individuality. The lyrics delve into various concepts that contribute to this overarching theme, giving the song depth and a thought-provoking nature. Let's explore some standout lyrics and discuss the creative and imaginative themes they help develop.One lyric that catches attention is, "Hey, miss, I'm your Mr. Miyagi. I'ma come and kick it in the Balenciagi." At first glance, this lyric may seem like a simple statement of confidence and style. However, a deeper interpretation could reveal a theme of personal growth and mentorship. Mr. Miyagi, a character from the movie "The Karate Kid," represents a mentor figure who teaches the protagonist important life lessons. In the song, the mention of Mr. Miyagi suggests that the speaker sees themselves as a guiding force for the addressee, encouraging personal growth and offering support.Another lyric that stands out is, "If I dig in my bag, it's an M. 'Bout to put a lil' bitch on a film. I done took off in air, I can't see him. Bound to geekin' before the codeine." These lines may seem disconnected at first, but they touch on themes of ambition, success, and the consequences of excess. The metaphorical "bag" symbolizes the speaker's resources and capabilities, with the "M" representing a substantial amount of money. By referencing putting someone on a film, it suggests capturing their attention or recognition through their actions. The allusion to taking off in the air signifies rising above others' expectations and limitations. However, the line "bound to geekin' before the codeine" introduces the idea that excessive indulgence may cloud judgment and hinder progress. It serves as a cautionary reminder within the song's theme of empowerment.Continuing on, Kanye West's verse provides another thought-provoking lyric: "I don't fuck with negatives. I don't need the therapy." This lyric explores the theme of personal positivity and self-assurance. By refusing to engage with negativity, the speaker emphasizes their commitment to maintaining a positive mindset. In this context, therapy represents a tool to overcome negative experiences and emotions. However, by asserting that they don't need therapy, the verse suggests a sense of self-reliance and inner strength.Playboi Carti's verse also contributes to the song's themes. The line "Rockstar stunts the navy jean jacket ho, bitch" showcases a theme of rebellion and nonconformity. The speaker embraces a rockstar persona, defying traditional norms and expectations. This lyric emphasizes individuality and the freedom to express oneself without hesitation or concern for societal judgment.In Kanye West's second verse, the lyric "Run the summer again, take my number again" introduces a theme of reinvention and second chances. The speaker desires to reclaim a past period of success and significance. By urging someone to take their number again, it implies a desire for renewed connection and the opportunity to rewrite history. This idea of seizing opportunities for reinvention aligns with the song's theme of empowerment and personal growth.Future's "Mr. Miyagi" explores themes of empowerment, individuality, personal growth, and the consequences of excess. Through various standout lyrics, each contributing to these themes in imaginative ways, the song encourages listeners to embrace their unique qualities, mentor others, remain positive in the face of negativity, and seek opportunities for personal reinvention. Ultimately, "Mr. Miyagi" serves as a powerful anthem, inspiring listeners to embrace their inner strengths and strive for success on their own terms.
