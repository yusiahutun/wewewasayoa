    Life begins at 38! Age at which adults feel their most content is getting younger

    The belief that life begins at 40 has just been challenged by a new study that showed Britons feel most content with their life at the age of 38 and generally enjoy a more comfortable existence during their 30s.

    People feel most confident in social situations such as bars, clubs and parties when they are in their mid-30s, a survey of 2,000 adults showed.

    The lifestyle research revealed that twice as many people said money was more important to them than friends, while most of those questioned said they would not swap their life with one of their friends.

    Social confidence: People feel most confident in social situations such as bars, clubs and parties when they are in their mid-30s, the survey showed

    More than one in four of those surveyed said they did not feel in control of their own lives, and two out of five worried about getting old.

    Married people or those with a partner said they felt most content at the age of 42, while those who have not tied the knot said true contentment came at 27.

    Women also were the most content and at ease with their bodies at the age of 31, while men felt most body confident at 30.

    But both the sexes felt their sexual confidence peaked when they turned 35.

    The most satisfactory balance of work and family life was achieved at 34, but 41-year-olds thought they made the most of life more so than any other age group.

    Twentysomethings were also more likely to be envious of their friends and cared more about money than their health, according to the survey undertaken by The Huffington Post UK.

    The figures contradict an academic study published earlier this year, which found that happiness throughout life is a ‘u-shaped curve’.

    An economist at Maastricht University in Belgium, Bert van Landeghem, said that a person’s life satisfaction drops in their late 20s and doesn’t pick back up until after 50, after conducting a study.

    Landeghem's research showed people appeared to be more satisfied with life when they reached middle age because their family life and careers became more stable.

    The most recent survey corresponded with that theory though, after finding that married couples were more content than their single counterparts.

    Those having a stable partner not worrying as much about getting older or wanting to swap their lives.
