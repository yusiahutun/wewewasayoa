Bluegrass pioneer George Shuffler died at the age of 88 on Monday (April 7). Shuffler -- born in Valdese, N.C. on April 15, 1925 -- was known as 'The Third Stanley Brother,' because he toured with the Stanley Brothers (Carter and Ralph) for 20 years. It was during that time he developed his trademark cross picking guitar style that has influenced many musicians, making an enduring contribution to bluegrass. Crosspicking enabled him to produce rhythmic and melodic elements simultaneously.
