Coronavirus: China's central bank cleaning notes with UV light to combat Covid-19 - YP

11 Sep 2024, 00:00

China’s central bank said in February it was disinfecting and isolating used banknotes using UV lights in its efforts to combat the coronavirus. Banks use ultraviolet light or high temperatures to disinfect yuan bills, then seal and store the cash for seven to 14 days – depending on the severity of the outbreak in a particular region – before recirculating them, China’s central bank said at a press conference. The virus has sparked a rush to disinfect public places and minimise contact between people.
