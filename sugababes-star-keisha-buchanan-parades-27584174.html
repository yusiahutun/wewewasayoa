    Sugababes pop princess Keisha Buchanan looked sensational as she poured her hourglass figure into a clinging bodysuit.

    The stunner donned the white long-sleeved piece, dotted with Nike logos and unbuttoned to show a hint of cleavage as she lounged on the back of an immaculate white couch.

    Her bodysuit was rouched in at the waist to draw even more attention to her incredible figure, and tucked into a pair of crushed velvet stiletto boots - and Keisha clearly wasn’t bothered about mucking up the furniture, as she lounged with her shoes up on the seat.

    The Red Dress singer let her dark locks fall down past her waist as she gazed off to the side, surrounded by lush cream curtains as she enjoyed a day at York Racecourse.

    A bold make-up look completed her ensemble, featuring a glam pink glossy lip and a dark smokey eye as Keisha turned up the heat in the private lounge.

    “The soul has to be as dope as the outfit by the way,” she captioned the snap.

    Fans immediately flocked to the comments section to praise Keisha’s look, with X Factor winner Alexandra Burke simply writing: “Girl!” with two heart-eyed emojis.

    Someone else branded her “beautiful”, as a third fan penned: “Nahhh I wasn’t ready for this! #TheHeat!”

    “You look amazing,” another social media user told her, as someone else echoed: “Now that’s an outfit!”

    Keisha was joined by her bandmates Mutya Buena and Siobhan Donaghy for their performance at the races this weekend, alongside music legends Madness.

    The girl band recently announced their first headline tour in more than 20 years, to take place this October with 17 dates across the UK.

    Making their announcement, Sugababes told fans: “We’re so excited to come full circle and announce our own headline UK tour.

    “We’ll be hitting the road in October and November - can’t wait to see you all there.”

    Her co-star Siobhan initially said that the Covid-19 pandemic had “thrown a spanner in the works”, explaining: “We were recording before the series of lockdowns in the past year, but we’ve got so much material that we need to work through.

    “We probably need to do a little more recording. As soon as we can get back together in the studio, we’re going to work on something fresh.”

    She added that the new tracks are “some of our best work ever” and that the band is intent on releasing them.
