    Commercial sex workers in Busia are feeling the pinch of the ongoing shortage of condoms in various parts of the country.

    According to Nation, sex workers in the border town are forced to use one condom to service multiple clients, clients, collect used condoms from dustbins which they wash and reuse to protect themselves and their clients.

    The revelation comes weeks after various organisations in the health sector disclosed the country had run out of the commodity.

    A lady of the night identified as Eunice revealed she once looked for a condom to use with a regular client before resorting to using one from the dustbin.

    "I asked my friend for a condom and she didn't have one. I picked a used condom from the dustbin and washed it. I did this to protect myself," Nation reported.

    She narrated that the condom later burst but they continued with their business and went to a health facility to take PrEP to prevent herself from getting HIV.

    Empty dispensers

    Sylvia Epalat, a nurse at Busia Survivors Organization confirmed the shortage in the county adding that most condom dispensers are empty.

    Epalat stated that said since the COVID-19 outbreak in 2020, the county has had to battle with a scarcity of condoms.

    "Condom dispensers in the county are empty," said Epalat.

    Busia Director of Health, Janerose Amoit Ambuchi added there was a growing need to have a budget to buy condoms in the transit town.

    "We now need to have budgets to buy condoms. For commercial sex workers, routine condom use is a priority. The poverty index in Busia is at 83% and this makes the key populations unable to buy condoms," said Amoit.

    Parts of Kenya hit by condom shortage

    In November 2022, a biting shortage of condoms hit Bungoma and Nairobi counties as the festive season drew close.

    The County Aid Control Officer, Fred Barasa said the situation was dire, making it hard for youths to protect themselves against sexually transmitted diseases.

    Speaking in Nairobi, AIDS Healthcare Foundation Kenya County Director Dr Samuel Kinyanjui warned that Nairobi and Nakuru are also affected by condom shortages.
